**Version**: 1.0
**Last Updated**: January 4, 2026

---

## How to Use Industry Bursts

SAM uses industry-specific conversation starters to:

1. Show immediate relevance when chatting with prospects
2. Reference pain points that resonate
3. Use language that signals expertise
4. Ask questions that unlock insights

---

## SaaS / Technology

**Opening Hooks**:

- "What's your current CAC payback period? I've seen it balloon for a lot of SaaS companies recently."
- "Are you seeing PLG cannibalize your outbound motion, or complement it?"
- "The 'Rule of 40' pressure seems intense right now. Where are you optimizing?"

**Pain Points to Reference**:

- NRR/churn pressure from economic uncertainty
- Sales efficiency metrics under scrutiny
- Longer sales cycles with more stakeholders
- CFO involvement earlier in purchase decisions

**Language to Use**:

- ARR, MRR, NRR, CAC, LTV
- Product-led vs sales-led
- Land and expand
- Net revenue retention

---

## FinTech

**Opening Hooks**:

- "Regulatory complexity seems to double every year. How are you staying ahead?"
- "Are you seeing enterprises finally open up to fintech, or still hesitant?"
- "What's your biggest friction point in the sales cycle - security review or compliance?"

**Pain Points to Reference**:

- SOC 2 and compliance as table stakes
- Bank partnership complexity
- Integration with legacy systems
- Building trust with risk-averse buyers

**Language to Use**:

- AML/KYC
- Open banking, API-first
- Embedded finance
- Regulatory sandbox

## Usage Guidelines

### When to Use

- Opening conversation to show relevance
- Responding to industry-specific questions
- Crafting personalized outreach messages
- Building credibility in discovery calls

### When NOT to Use

- If you don't know the prospect's industry
- If the jargon feels forced or unnatural
- If the prospect is clearly in a different segment
