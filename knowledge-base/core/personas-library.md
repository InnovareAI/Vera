## Overview

SAM adapts communication style based on the buyer persona. This library contains detailed profiles for C-level executives commonly targeted in B2B sales.

---

## CFO (Chief Financial Officer)

### Personality

- **Analytical** — Needs data to support decisions
- **ROI-driven** — Every investment must justify itself
- **Compliance-focused** — Risk-averse, regulatory-aware
- **Time-conscious** — Wants bottom-line quickly

### Priorities

1. Cost savings and efficiency
2. Risk reduction
3. Compliance and audit readiness
4. Measurable ROI

### Pain Points

- Budget overruns
- Unpredictable costs
- Compliance gaps
- Poor visibility into spend

### How to Approach

- Lead with numbers (ROI, cost savings, payback period)
- Reference compliance frameworks
- Provide benchmarks ("Companies your size typically spend X")
- Be concise — get to the point fast

### Sample Opening

```
"CFOs I talk to are focused on [specific metric]. We've helped 
similar companies see X% improvement in Y. Worth a quick look?"
```

## CTO (Chief Technology Officer)

### Personality

- **Technical** — Wants to understand how things work
- **Precise** — Details matter
- **Strategic** — Thinks long-term about architecture
- **Security-conscious** — Protective of systems

### Priorities

1. Security and compliance
2. Integration with existing stack
3. Scalability and performance
4. Technical team efficiency

### Pain Points

- Technical debt
- Integration complexity
- Security vulnerabilities
- Talent shortage

### How to Approach

- Be technically accurate
- Respect their expertise
- Discuss architecture and integration
- Address security upfront

### Sample Opening

```
"CTOs in [industry] are dealing with [technical challenge]. 
We integrate via [method] and handle [security concern]. 
Curious if that resonates?"
```

## COO (Chief Operating Officer)

### Personality

- **Operational** — Focused on execution
- **Practical** — Skeptical of theory, wants proof
- **Efficiency-driven** — Always looking to optimize
- **Resilience-focused** — Thinks about what could go wrong

### Priorities

1. Operational efficiency
2. Process reliability
3. Scalability
4. Business continuity

### Pain Points

- Bottlenecks and delays
- Manual processes
- Inconsistent output
- Capacity constraints

### How to Approach

- Focus on operational improvements
- Show process before/after
- Quantify time savings
- Address reliability and uptime

### Sample Opening

```
"COOs I work with are trying to [operational challenge]. 
We've helped teams like yours [specific improvement]. 
Sound relevant?"
```

## CMO (Chief Marketing Officer)

### Personality

- **Data-driven** — Measures everything
- **Creative** — Values innovation and differentiation
- **Multi-channel** — Thinks across touchpoints
- **Attribution-focused** — Needs to prove marketing's value

### Priorities

1. Campaign ROI and attribution
2. CAC:LTV optimization
3. Brand consistency
4. Pipeline contribution

### Pain Points

- Attribution challenges
- Rising CAC
- Content fatigue
- Proving marketing's impact to the board

### How to Approach

- Lead with metrics (pipeline, revenue attribution)
- Show channel-specific results
- Discuss personalization at scale
- Reference brand consistency

### Sample Opening

```
"CMOs in [industry] are seeing CAC rise [X%]. We help 
marketing teams lower acquisition cost through [approach]. 
Would that be useful to explore?"
```

## CHRO (Chief Human Resources Officer)

### Personality

- **Supportive** — Focused on employee experience
- **People-first** — Every decision impacts people
- **Strategic** — Connects talent to business outcomes
- **Culture-conscious** — Protective of company culture

### Priorities

1. Employee retention and engagement
2. Talent pipeline and recruiting
3. DEI and culture
4. HR tech efficiency

### Pain Points

- High turnover
- Recruiting competition
- Employee burnout
- Disconnected HR systems

### How to Approach

- Focus on people outcomes
- Show impact on retention/engagement
- Discuss employee experience
- Be sensitive to culture concerns

### Sample Opening

```
"CHROs are telling me retention is [challenge]. We've helped 
HR teams [specific outcome]. Could be relevant for you?"
```

### Priorities

2. Win rate improvement
3. Sales cycle reduction
4. Rep productivity

### Pain Points

- Pipeline gaps
- Long sales cycles
- Low conversion rates
- Rep ramp time

- Discuss competitive wins

### Sample Opening

```
"VPs of Sales I talk to are focused on [metric]. We've helped 
teams add [X%] to pipeline. Quick call to see if relevant?"
```

## VP of Engineering

### Personality

- **Technical** — Deep expertise, respects competence
- **Protective** — Guards the team from distractions
- **Efficient** — Cares about developer productivity
- **Quality-focused** — Hates tech debt

### Priorities

1. Developer productivity
2. System reliability
3. Tech debt reduction
4. Hiring and retention

### Pain Points

- Too many tools
- Context switching
- Technical debt
- Recruiting engineers

### How to Approach

- Be technical but concise
- Respect their time
- Focus on developer experience
- Show integration clarity

### Sample Opening

```
"VPs of Engineering are dealing with [challenge]. We help 
reduce [specific friction]. Worth a quick look?"
```

## Quick Reference

| Persona | Lead With | Avoid |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| CFO | ROI, cost savings, benchmarks | Vague claims, no numbers |
| CTO | Security, integration, architecture | Over-simplification, buzzwords |
| COO | Efficiency, process, reliability | Theoretical, unproven |
| CMO | Attribution, CAC, pipeline | Unmeasurable claims |
| CHRO | Retention, experience, culture | Ignoring people impact |
| VP Sales | Pipeline, win rate, productivity | Fluff, slow build-up |
| VP Engineering | Developer experience, integration | Sales speak, interruptions |
